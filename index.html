Total compensation calculator

Base salary alone is misleading. Model the full True Compensation Value (TCV) — including equity, benefits, brand premium, and lifestyle economics.

How this is derived: Calculates True Compensation Value (TCV) by summing Base Salary, Annual Bonus, Equity (RSUs), Amortized Sign-On Bonus (divided by 3 years), Retirement Match, and Estimated Benefits. The sum is then multiplied by a tier-based brand premium. Remote savings are estimated at $5,200/year per remote day.

Cost-of-living normalizer

Compare two offers on adjusted terms. True purchasing power tells the real story of compensation.

How this is derived: Normalizes nominal compensation using regional Cost of Living indices (US average = 100) and subtracts top marginal state/local tax rates. This reveals true purchasing power, clearly modeling how a competitive Salt Lake City offer frequently outperforms an inflated coastal salary when market failures and tax drags are removed.

Offer evaluation scorecard

Rate two offers 1–5 across 8 weighted dimensions. The weighted total reveals the stronger long-term strategic choice.

How this is derived: Calculates a weighted total out of 100 based on subjective 1–5 user inputs across eight distinct career dimensions. The weights strictly prioritize long-term trajectory over immediate nominal compensation.

Skill premium index

Skills are market assets. Compensation is their market price. Use this to prioritize upskilling for maximum compensation ROI.

How this is derived: Indexes skillsets based on current labor market demand and expected compensation lift premiums. Scores are derived from live, aggregated salary transparency datasets (such as Levels.fyi, Glassdoor, and H1B visa reporting) and continuous employer signals.
